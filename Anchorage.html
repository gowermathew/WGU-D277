Quick Facts

  • Largest city in Alaska
  • Home to around 40% of the state's population
  • Founded in 1914 as a railroad construction port
  • Has a subarctic climate
  • Known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreational opportunities

Overview

Anchorage is the most populous city in Alaska, with a population of 291,247 according to the 2020 census. The city contains nearly 40% of the state’s population, making it the primary urban hub of Alaska. Incorporated as a city in 1920, Anchorage is located in southcentral Alaska on a peninsula formed by Knik Arm and Turnagain Arm. It is considered an urban city and serves as a cultural and economic center for the region. Households in Anchorage earn a median income of roughly $98,000 per year, which is higher than the overall Alaskan average. Despite being a modern city, Anchorage is famous for its close connection to nature—moose, black bears, and even bald eagles are commonly seen within city limits, reflecting Alaska’s unique blend of urban life and wilderness.
